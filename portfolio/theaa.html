Vixa: Always Moving, Always Connected

Designing the ultimate connected car platform that transforms vehicle data into actionable insights, preventing breakdowns and empowering modern drivers.

Connected Car Platform

Real-time vehicle diagnostics, proactive maintenance alerts, and seamless service integration through a physical plugin and mobile app ecosystem.

Project Timeline

12-month development cycle from concept to market launch, with continuous iteration based on real-world driving data and user feedback.

Key Objective

Reduce unexpected breakdowns by 70% and extend average vehicle lifespan through predictive maintenance and driver education.

THE CHALLENGE

Modern Driving, Outdated Maintenance

Drivers today face unpredictable breakdowns, confusing dashboard warnings, and reactive maintenance that costs time, money, and creates unnecessary stress.

The Connected Car Gap

While vehicles generate thousands of data points every minute, drivers lack accessible insights until something goes wrong. Key challenges include:

1

Reactive Maintenance

85% of drivers only address issues after warning lights appear, leading to more expensive repairs and increased breakdown risk.

2

Data Without Context

Modern vehicles produce extensive diagnostics data, but without translation into actionable insights, it remains inaccessible to most drivers.

3

Service Fragmentation

Finding trustworthy mechanics, scheduling appointments, and understanding repair recommendations creates unnecessary friction.

Key Research Insights

Through synthesis of workshop findings and user interviews, we identified critical patterns that would shape our design strategy.

Peace of Mind Priority

Drivers value confidence in their vehicle's reliability more than cost savings. The emotional burden of breakdown anxiety is significant.

Data Transparency

Users want to understand their vehicle's health in simple terms, not technical jargon. Visualizations beat raw data.

Trust Deficit

Many drivers distrust repair recommendations due to past experiences with unnecessary upsells or unclear explanations.

Key Learnings

This project revealed important insights about designing connected hardware-software ecosystems for everyday users.

Seamless Hardware Integration

The physical installation experience must be as refined as the digital interface. One friction point in setup can undermine trust in the entire system.

Translating Technical Data

The greatest challenge wasn't collecting vehicle data, but presenting it in ways that are immediately understandable and actionable for non-experts.

Ecosystem Partnerships

Success depended on building strong relationships with repair shops and service centers to create a truly integrated end-to-end experience.

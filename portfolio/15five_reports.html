Airbnb Booking Experience Redesign

A comprehensive redesign of Airbnb's checkout flow to reduce friction, increase transparency, and improve conversion rates through user-centered design principles.

My Role

Product Designer responsible for user research, interaction design, prototyping, and usability testing.

The Team

5 Engineers and 1 Product Manager working collaboratively in agile sprints.

Timeline

4-month project from discovery to launch, with ongoing iteration based on user feedback and analytics.

THE CHALLENGE

From Checkout Friction to Seamless Booking

Users experienced significant friction during Airbnb's booking and checkout process, with analytics showing high drop-off rates and user feedback indicating confusion around pricing transparency and trust signals.

Key Problem Areas

Through user interviews and analytics review, we identified three core issues preventing users from completing bookings:

1

High Drop-off Rate

42% of users abandoned the checkout process before completing payment, with most exits occurring at the payment information screen.

2

Pricing Confusion

Users reported uncertainty about total costs, with hidden fees and charges appearing late in the booking process causing frustration.

3

Trust Concerns

First-time users expressed hesitation about entering payment information without clear security indicators and host verification.

After establishing target participant criteria, we conducted in-depth interviews with users matching our persona profiles. Following the interviews, we transcribed each session and extracted individual observations, organizing them into thematic categories through affinity mapping.

Interview guide and affinity mapping process showing thematic categorization of observations

Complete Price Transparency

Users wanted to see the complete price breakdown upfront, including all fees, taxes, and charges before reaching the payment screen.

Simplified Checkout Flow

Multi-step forms felt overwhelming. Users preferred a single-page checkout with progressive disclosure of details.

Mobile-First Optimization

Mobile users struggled with form inputs, especially date pickers and payment information fields that weren't optimized for touch.

IDEATION & STRATEGY

From Insights to Design Solutions

We transformed research findings into actionable design principles and features through collaborative workshops and iterative prototyping.

Design Principles

We established four core design principles to guide our solution development and ensure consistency throughout the redesign.

Transparency First

Show complete pricing breakdown early in the flow. No surprises at checkout.

Reduce Friction

Minimize form fields and steps. Use smart defaults and saved information.

Build Trust

Display security badges, host verification, and cancellation policies clearly.

Mobile Optimized

Design for thumb-friendly interactions and touch-optimized input fields.

User Journey & Information Architecture

We mapped complete user journeys and redesigned information architecture to create a seamless booking experience from discovery to confirmation.

Core User Story

As an Airbnb user, I want to book an accommodation in the app and share it with other users, so I can save money as this functionality is more affordable.

I want to share the accommodation with like-minded people, based on the likes and dislikes I have filled in my Airbnb profile.

I want to see how many spaces are available and occupied by accommodation and contact the other users if they already have a confirmed reservation.

Note: The host always has the last word and is the one who has to accept the reservations of all users.

Key Learnings

This project reinforced several important principles for designing effective e-commerce experiences:

Transparency Builds Trust

Showing complete pricing information early in the flow significantly reduced user anxiety and increased completion rates. Users appreciated knowing the full cost before investing time in the checkout process.

Less is More

Consolidating multiple steps into a single, well-organized page reduced cognitive load and made the process feel faster and simpler, even though users were providing the same information.

Mobile-First Matters

Optimizing for mobile interactions wasn't just about responsive design—it required rethinking input methods, touch targets, and the entire flow to match how users actually interact with their devices.

Iterate with Real Users

Regular usability testing throughout the design process helped us catch issues early and validate solutions before development. User feedback was invaluable in refining the experience.

Redesigning the EasyJet Booking Experience

A UX case study: Enhancing the flight booking journey for Europe's leading low-cost airline to stay competitive and improve user satisfaction.

My Role

My Team

Product Manager, QA Engineers, Frontend & Backend Developers, UX Researchers.

Project Duration

5 months from initial research to final implementation.

PROJECT OVERVIEW

Modernizing Europe's Leading Budget Airline

The Challenge

EasyJet, being one of the leading budget airlines globally, was facing a challenge with its booking experience. The current website was lagging behind its competitors, who are providing a more seamless and user-friendly booking process. As a result, EasyJet needed a redesign to enhance its booking experience and stay competitive in the market.

Key Issues Identified

1

Usability Concerns

Users encountered difficulties in navigating the website, finding flights, and completing the booking process due to usability issues and lack of intuitive design.

2

Outdated Interface

The website's interface appeared dated compared to competitors, leading to a less engaging and visually appealing user experience.

3

Complex Booking Process

The booking process was cumbersome, with multiple steps and unclear instructions, leading to user frustration and drop-offs.

4

Limited Personalization

Lack of personalized recommendations and tailored experiences resulted in users not feeling fully engaged with the booking process.

The Goal

To redesign EasyJet's booking experience with a focus on usability, modern aesthetics, and a streamlined booking flow that reduces friction and improves conversion rates while maintaining the brand's identity as a leading low-cost airline.

Understanding Traveler Needs & Pain Points

Conducted a thorough design review of the existing website to identify usability issues, navigation problems, and areas for improvement.

Customer Surveys

Competitor Analysis

Design Review & Heuristic Analysis

We conducted a comprehensive design review as the foundational approach to understanding the current state of EasyJet's website. This systematic evaluation allowed us to identify critical usability issues, assess visual design effectiveness, and establish a solid foundation for the redesign process. By applying established UX heuristics, we uncovered both surface-level problems and deeper structural issues impacting the user experience.

Comprehensive design review highlighting usability issues and improvement opportunities

Complex & Cluttered Layout

The interface suffered from information overload, making it difficult for users to find essential information quickly. Multiple competing visual elements created cognitive strain.

Confusing Navigation

Users struggled with unclear menu structures and inconsistent navigation patterns. Important sections were buried deep within the information architecture.

Accessibility Gaps

The website lacked proper accessibility features, excluding users with disabilities. Poor color contrast and missing ARIA labels created barriers to access.

Weak Visual Hierarchy

Call-to-action buttons didn't stand out, reducing conversion rates. Important information wasn't prioritized visually, causing users to miss critical details.

Research Findings

Our research revealed consistent patterns across different user groups. Travelers expressed frustration with complex navigation, unclear pricing, and a booking process that felt outdated compared to competitor platforms. Key pain points included:

Key Usability Issues Identified

Complex and Cluttered Layout: The page had a complex and cluttered layout, making it challenging for users to quickly find essential information.
Confusing Navigation: Users faced difficulties in finding relevant sections due to unclear navigation menus.
Lack of Accessibility Features: The page was not fully accessible to users with disabilities, excluding a significant audience.
Unclear Call-to-Action (CTA): CTAs did not stand out clearly, resulting in reduced conversion rates.

User Personas

Based on our research, we created two primary personas representing EasyJet's key customer segments:

Goals

Find suitable connections, comfortable travel experience, flexibility to change plans, reasonable pricing.

Vacationer | Age 27

Information Architecture

To reorganize the site structure based on user mental models, we conducted card sorting exercises with representative users. This helped us create an intuitive information architecture that aligns with how users think about flight booking.

Card sorting results informing the new information architecture and sitemap

Card Sorting Results

Users consistently grouped features into logical categories: Booking & Tickets, Travel Information, Journey Planning, and Company Information. This informed our new sitemap and navigation structure, making it easier for users to find what they need without confusion.

The exercise revealed that users preferred clear, descriptive category names over marketing jargon. They also expected to find related features grouped together, such as "Manage Booking" and "Check-in" being in the same section.

User Flow Optimization

One of the main pain points identified was the complexity of the ticket booking process. We mapped the existing user flow and identified opportunities to reduce steps, clarify instructions, and create a more streamlined experience. The user flow diagram illustrates each step a user takes from initial search to completed booking, highlighting decision points and potential drop-off areas.

Key Flow Insights

Redundant Steps

The original flow contained 4 unnecessary confirmation screens that increased cognitive load.

Decision Paralysis

Users faced too many choices at critical junctures, leading to analysis paralysis and abandonment.

Poor Error Recovery

Error states didn't provide clear guidance, forcing users to start over instead of correcting mistakes.

Ideation & Sketches

We began with Crazy Eights exercises to generate diverse ideas quickly. Since the website's main functionality is searching, booking tickets, and online check-in, our focus was on providing an uncomplicated search experience.

Mid-Fidelity Wireframes

Once we had a visual direction, we added more details to sketches by turning them into mid-fidelity wireframes. These incorporated elements addressing user goals while using common airline design patterns.

High-Fidelity Prototype

We created a fully interactive prototype in Figma, incorporating EasyJet's branding, refined interactions, and a complete booking flow for usability testing.

Ideation & Sketches

Our design process followed a user-centered approach, with each iteration informed by research findings and usability testing results. Visual Design Principles

Our visual design approach was guided by several key principles that emerged from our research:

Clarity Over Cleverness

Prioritized clear communication over decorative elements that could confuse users.

Consistent Branding

Maintained EasyJet's orange and white color scheme while improving contrast and accessibility.

Progressive Disclosure

Revealed information gradually to prevent overwhelming users with too many choices at once.

USABILITY TESTING

Validating Design Decisions

With the first prototype ready, we conducted moderated usability testing with 6 participants. The goal was for them to complete a full flight booking. While the app incorporated common design patterns from other airline apps, we needed to validate whether our new functions would effectively address the challenges identified during research.

Interactive Prototype

We created a fully interactive prototype that simulated the complete flight booking experience, from search to payment confirmation. This allowed test participants to experience the redesigned flow as if it were a real application.

Complete Booking Flow

The prototype included the entire booking journey from flight search to payment confirmation.

Realistic Interactions

Interactive elements mimicked real app behavior, including form validation and feedback.

Usability Testing

The prototype was used in usability studies to identify pain points and areas for improvement.

EasyJet Interactive Prototype

Interactive prototype would be embedded here in a real implementation

Key Findings

Flight Search Form: All users managed to fill the form with ease. Initially, we were worried the graph calendar view would pose problems, but we received positive feedback and a 100% success rate for date selection.
Flight Selection: More than half of participants missed the "sort by" button when browsing flight results as they found it hard to distinguish. Interestingly, while all users mentioned this button was handy, half wouldn't have used it as they preferred browsing and comparing results.
Trip Review & Payment: Half of the users were interested in reading the terms & conditions, however there was no option to expand them. The trip details, condensed in sub-menus, were easily understood. In the payment screen, some users expected to have the ability to scan their card.
DESIGN REFINEMENTS

Iterating Based on User Feedback

Based on usability study findings, we made several key improvements to the design:

1

Improved Clickable Elements

Users found it difficult to distinguish between clickable items and plain text. We enhanced button styling and link visibility to reduce cognitive load.

2

Clearer Selection Indicators

We added visual feedback for selectable options, including hover states and selection indicators, to help users navigate more confidently.

3

Enhanced Flight Sorting

We redesigned the "sort by" functionality to be more visible and intuitive, addressing the issue where more than half of users missed it initially.

4

Expanded Payment Options

Based on user feedback, we added card scanning functionality and made terms & conditions more accessible with expandable sections.

RESULTS & LEARNINGS

Impact and Key Takeaways

What We Learned

The redesign process taught us valuable lessons about user behavior and design effectiveness:

Clickable Elements Recognition

Users need clear visual cues to identify interactive elements. Buttons and links should be prominently highlighted to reduce cognitive load.

Selection Feedback Importance

Visual feedback for selections (hover states, active states) significantly improves navigation confidence and reduces errors.

Design Impact on Engagement

A general improvement in platform UI had a positive impact on user retention, conversion rate, and Net Promoter Score (NPS).

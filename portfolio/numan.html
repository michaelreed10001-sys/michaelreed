Key Insights

Four root causes of early churn

Synthesising all research streams, we identified four compounding problems driving the 28% pre-R2 drop-off.

Patient journey — first 8 weeks on GLP-1 treatment

Critical moments where engagement is won or lost

1
Delivery & Download
Day 1–3
First injection. App download prompted. Weight verification required. No clear onboarding goal set.
2
Early Side Effects
Week 1–2 ⚠️
Nausea, fatigue common. Patients unsure what's normal. High contact rate to CC. 8% cite side effects as churn reason.
3
No Visible Results
Week 2–4 ⚠️
GLP-1s suppress appetite gradually. Patients expect fast results. If weight not tracked, progress invisible. Motivation dips.
4
Repeat 2 Decision
Week 4–6
Next payment due. 28% cancel here. Cost vs perceived value calculation. Low engagement = low perceived value.
5
Habitual Engagement
Week 6–8
Patients who make it here show habits forming. Coach relationship developing. Retention curve plateaus.
01

Poor Onboarding

Patients downloaded the app to verify weight, not because they understood its value. No clear onboarding walkthrough set expectations for how the app supports treatment.

02

Invisible Progress

Without regular weight logging, patients couldn't see their own progress. Non-scale victories (energy, sleep, hunger reduction) went untracked and uncelebrated.

03

Coaching Perceived as Generic

67% of patients have the app, 60% have a message — but most assume coaching will be generic. Only ~18,000 of ~30,000 active patients are actively coached.

04

Side Effect Anxiety

No in-app side effects guidance in the critical first two weeks. Patients self-serve via CC (7,000 interactions/week) or simply stop treatment.

Design Solutions

What we built to fix it

Three interconnected design interventions to drive engagement in the critical first 28 days — targeting the moments most predictive of long-term retention.

Redesigned Onboarding
Goal-setting · Expectation framing · First 7 days
Guided onboarding sets a personalised weight goal and 28-day milestone on first open
Side-effect explainer shown proactively in Week 1 ("What to expect this week") before patients experience anxiety
Coaching intro video reframes coaching as "your personal programme" not generic support
Lifestyle Questionnaire surfaced within 48hrs, framed as personalising the programme
Progress Visibility System
Weight tracking · Non-scale victories · Weekly summaries
Weekly check-in nudge with streak gamification to build a logging habit within Week 1
Non-scale victory tracking (energy, hunger, sleep, mood) ensures visible progress even before weight shifts
Weekly progress summary sent in-app and via push notification with encouragement framing
Weight trend visualisation shows "direction of travel" even when fluctuations mask weekly progress
Smart Coach Activation
Triggered prompts · Personalised coaching · Engagement scoring
Behaviour-triggered coach messages based on engagement signals (e.g. no log in 5 days = proactive outreach)
Lifestyle Questionnaire responses automatically routed to coach for personalised follow-up within 24hrs
Coach response time SLA displayed in-app to set expectations and build trust
Side-effect check-in flow routes directly to clinical support or coach depending on severity

Reflections

What I'd do differently

→

Earlier Patient Interviews

Survey data told us the "what" but not always the "why". I would have run 1:1 interviews with churned patients in week one rather than week three of discovery.

→

Coach Team Earlier

Coaches had enormous insight into patient friction. Involving them from the Define phase rather than just the handoff phase would have unlocked more nuanced solutions.

→

Push Notification Strategy

We underestimated how much engagement is driven outside the app. A full push notification strategy should have been in scope from the start, not a follow-on workstream.

→

Long-term Habit Research

Our research was strongest in weeks 1–4. We need a dedicated research strand for months 3–6 to understand what drives habit formation and prevents later-stage churn.

Numan Health — App Retention Case Study

This case study demonstrates how user-centred research and a data-informed design process can unlock meaningful retention improvements in a high-stakes healthcare context. By diagnosing the root causes of early churn and designing targeted interventions for the critical first 28 days, we helped more patients stay on treatment long enough to see life-changing results.

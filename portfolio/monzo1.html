Project overview

Monzo's mission is to make money work for everyone. This case study covers two parallel work streams: rebuilding the core payments flow (sending/requesting money) and designing internal tools for support teams to handle disputes efficiently.

The common thread: reducing friction and cognitive load — whether you're a customer splitting a bill or a support agent investigating a failed payment.

Operational design principles

Four principles guided every design decision across both the consumer payments overhaul and the internal tooling work — not as abstract values, but as active lenses applied to every screen, every flow, every edge case.

💰
Account funding
Principle 01

Multiple funding methods — bank transfer, debit card, top-up — must feel intuitive from first use. Delays in fund availability must be surfaced clearly, not hidden behind ambiguous status messages.

Transparency Speed Trust signals
⚙️
Account management
Principle 02

Everyday actions — sending money, splitting bills, checking balances — should feel effortless. Complexity is absorbed by the system, not pushed onto the user through unnecessary steps or ambiguous feedback.

Effortlessness Clarity Reduced friction
🔄
Money movement
Principle 03

The entire money flow — from external source to settled balance — was mapped end-to-end. Authorisation friction and settlement ambiguity were identified as the two primary failure points to resolve.

End-to-end thinking Seamlessness
🔒
Banking compliance
Principle 04

Every screen handling sensitive data was reviewed against PSD2, UKCA, and Monzo's internal security standards. Audit trails were a non-negotiable architectural requirement baked in from the first wireframe.

PSD2 compliant Audit trails Security-first

Stakeholder interviews

Before diving into user research, we ran a series of structured interviews with 11 internal stakeholders across product, engineering, compliance, and customer support. The goal was to surface competing priorities, known constraints, and organisational context that wouldn't emerge from user data alone.

Head of Money Movement

"Real-time settlement is the expectation now — any lag in fund availability directly erodes trust in the product."

Highlighted that payment rail latency was a top driver of support contacts, particularly for Faster Payments and international transfers.

Savings & Pots Product Lead

"Customers treat their Pots like separate accounts. The flow between main balance and Pots needs to feel instant and effortless."

Identified friction in moving money between Pots and the main account as a key contributor to drop-off in savings engagement.

Treasury & Liquidity Manager

"We need the UI to reflect the reality of settlement windows — overpromising instant availability for certain rails causes downstream issues."

Flagged that misleading status messaging around pending funds was creating reconciliation problems at the treasury level.

Open Banking & Integrations Lead

"Variable Recurring Payments are coming — the architecture we design now needs to support consent-based pull payments natively."

Surfaced the need to future-proof the payments IA for Open Banking-driven flows, which shaped how we structured the Move Money hub.

A key theme across all interviews: the boundary between storing and moving money is blurring. Customers expect Pots, transfers, and payment rails to work as one seamless system — not as separate features bolted together.

Competitor analysis — payments flows

We audited the send and request money flows across six major banks and fintechs, mapping step counts, contact selection patterns, authorisation methods, and fee transparency. This gave us a clear picture of where the market was setting expectations — and where gaps existed.

Revolut
Fintech · Global
Strengths

Highly streamlined send flow — 3 steps to complete a payment. Smart contact suggestions pull from phone contacts and Revolut network simultaneously. Multi-currency switching is seamless and inline.

Weaknesses

Fee disclosure is buried — users often only see charges post-confirmation. Request money flow requires recipients to have the app, limiting reach.

Opportunity for Monzo

Match the step count but lead on upfront fee transparency and app-agnostic request links.

Starling Bank
Neobank · UK
Strengths

Clean, minimal UI with clear payment confirmation screens. Strong use of payee photos and recent contacts. Splitting bills is straightforward with clear per-person breakdowns.

Weaknesses

No native pay-by-link or shareable request flow. International payments redirect to a third-party experience, breaking continuity.

Opportunity for Monzo

Offer a more complete end-to-end experience by keeping international transfers and shareable links native to the app.

Barclays
Traditional Bank · UK
Strengths

High trust for large transfers. Detailed confirmation screens with sortcode/account verification (CoP) clearly visible. Strong accessibility across age groups.

Weaknesses

Send flow averages 7–9 steps. Contact management is cumbersome — no smart suggestions or recents. Split and request features are absent entirely.

Opportunity for Monzo

Win users frustrated by legacy flows by dramatically reducing steps while maintaining the confirmation clarity they trust.

Wise
Fintech · International
Strengths

Best-in-class fee transparency — mid-market rate and exact fees shown before confirmation. Recipient lookup across 70+ countries. Clear estimated arrival times per rail.

Weaknesses

Domestic payments are secondary to the product — UK transfers feel bolted on. No bill-splitting or request-money flow. Onboarding friction is high for new recipients.

Opportunity for Monzo

Adopt Wise's fee transparency model for international transfers (already integrated via partnership) while wrapping it in Monzo's superior domestic UX.

HSBC
Traditional Bank · Global
Strengths

Robust payee management with detailed history. Global transfer capability is genuinely broad. Strong two-factor auth that users trust for high-value payments.

Weaknesses

Payment flow is fragmented — domestic, international and standing orders each live in separate areas. No bill-split or social payment features. Mobile UI lags significantly behind neobanks.

Opportunity for Monzo

Consolidate all payment types into a single unified hub — a clear differentiator for HSBC customers switching to Monzo.

Cash App
Fintech · US
Strengths

Fastest send flow in the market — 2 taps to pay a contact. $Cashtag system removes account detail friction entirely. Request flow is social and frictionless, with emoji support driving engagement.

Weaknesses

US-only for core payments. No international transfer capability. Weak dispute resolution and customer support infrastructure.

Opportunity for Monzo

Bring the social, low-friction request experience to a UK audience — particularly for the split bills and Monzo.me use cases.

How these themes shaped our research

  • Funding methods: We probed how users add money (direct debit, faster payments, card top‑ups) and uncovered that 28% were confused by delays in fund availability.
  • Account management: Users wanted clearer ways to organise money (e.g., pots, scheduled payments) – a key input for the new IA.
  • Money entry: We traced where money "enters" the system (inbound transfers, salary, cash deposits) and found that status updates were often unclear.
  • Data accuracy & compliance: Agents highlighted that manual data re‑entry led to errors; we logged requirements for automated, auditable records.

Identifying core problems

Before committing to any design direction, we synthesised everything gathered during the discovery phase — analytics, stakeholder interviews, support ticket themes, and competitor analysis — into a clear problem statement. This step ensured that subsequent design work was grounded in verified user frustrations rather than assumptions.

The core problem: Monzo's payments experience had grown organically over several years, resulting in a fragmented flow that required too many steps, surfaced authorisation and fee information inconsistently, and left agents without the unified tooling they needed to resolve disputes quickly and confidently.

Final solution

1. Payments experience overhaul

The redesigned flow reduces steps from 7 to 4. A new contact selector uses smart grouping (recent, frequent, by bank) and large avatars. Authorisation is unified: a single biometric/PIN prompt after all details are confirmed, with clear fee display for international payments. Users can now also schedule recurring payments.

International transfers

  • Powered by Wise: Monzo integrates with Wise to facilitate international payments in 38 currencies.
  • How to send: Tap Payments > Pay someone > Transfer internationally.
  • Fees: Monzo shows the exchange rate and any fees upfront. For receiving international payments in foreign currencies, a 1% conversion fee (capped at £1,000) typically applies.
  • Direct currencies: Popular currencies like USD, EUR, AUD, and INR can often be sent directly from the app without a separate Wise account setup.

Monzo Virtual Cards

Monzo virtual cards are digital-only debit cards that exist within your Monzo app rather than as a physical piece of plastic. They are primarily used to enhance security for online shopping and to assist with budgeting by allowing you to pay directly from specific Pots.

  • Types of virtual cards: You can create permanent virtual cards for regular subscriptions, one‑time use cards for extra security on single purchases, or cards that expire after 24 hours for added peace of mind.
  • Pay from Pots: Link a virtual card to a specific Pot, so spending is automatically deducted from that Pot – ideal for managing budgets like groceries, bills, or holiday spending.
  • Enhanced security: Because virtual cards are not physical, they can't be lost or stolen. You can freeze, unfreeze, or replace them instantly from the app.
  • Organise recurring payments: Assign a virtual card to each regular bill (e.g., Netflix, Spotify) to keep track of subscriptions and avoid missed payments.

Integration with other apps

  • Easy bank transfers: You can "pull" money into Monzo from other UK bank apps (like Barclays or HSBC) without manually entering details, using Open Banking.
  • Payment wallets: Monzo cards can be added to Apple Pay and Google Pay for instant spending and adding money.
  • Third‑party services: For US users, Monzo supports integration with Cash App, Venmo, and PayPal for sending and receiving funds.
  • Budgeting & aggregators: You can connect Monzo to apps like Emma, Snoop, and Revolut to view all your balances in one place.

2. Internal tooling (dispute management)

A dedicated "Ops Center" gives agents a 360° view: transaction timeline, customer details, and dispute history all in one pane. Actions like "temporary refund", "chargeback", and "freeze card" are one click away. The tool reduced average dispute handling time from 11 to 3.5 minutes.

"The new agent workspace is a game changer — I can resolve a dispute while on the phone without switching tabs." — Monzo support team lead

Final UI screens: Home, contact selection, payment sent (and additional placeholders).

Operational impact of the final design

  • Funding: The new "Move money" hub includes a dedicated top‑up section, showing all funding methods and real-time balance updates.
  • Account management: Smart recents, scheduled payments, and clearer pots integration give customers full control.
  • Money entry: Inbound payments now trigger push notifications with clear status (pending / received), reducing uncertainty.
  • Data accuracy & compliance: The internal Ops Center automatically logs every action taken on a dispute, creating an immutable audit trail that satisfies regulatory requirements.

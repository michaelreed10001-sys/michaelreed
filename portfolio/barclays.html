THE CHALLENGE

Modernizing Legacy Banking for the Digital Age

Barclays' existing mobile banking app was built on legacy infrastructure, creating a disjointed user experience that failed to meet modern customer expectations for seamless digital banking.

Key Problem Areas

User research and analytics revealed critical pain points preventing customers from effectively managing their finances through digital channels:

1

Complex Navigation

Deep menu structures and inconsistent IA made simple tasks like bill payments or balance checks unnecessarily complicated.

2

Accessibility Gaps

Critical WCAG 2.1 failures excluded users with disabilities, violating financial inclusion principles and regulatory requirements.

3

Security Friction

Overly complex authentication flows created abandonment rates of 23% during account login and transaction approval processes.

Key Research Insights

Accessibility is Essential

15% of Barclays customers reported accessibility challenges, with screen reader compatibility being the most critical issue.

Security vs. Convenience Balance

Users wanted robust security but without the complexity—biometric authentication was preferred by 78% of participants.

Personalized Financial Insights

Customers desired proactive financial guidance, not just transaction listings—especially around budgeting and saving.

User Personas

We identified four primary personas representing Barclays' diverse customer base across different life stages and banking needs.

Margaret Simmons

Retiree | Low Vision

Goals

Easy bill payments, accessible statements, and reliable fraud detection without complex digital interactions.

Pain Points

IDEATION & STRATEGY

From Research Insights to Concrete Solutions

We transformed research findings into actionable design directions through collaborative workshops and systematic design thinking approaches.

Ideation

We conducted a collaborative workshop to synthesize research insights and identify key focus areas for addressing critical user issues. The session prioritized design solutions around mortgage product visibility, eligibility criteria clarity, streamlined onboarding processes, accurate financial calculators, and comprehensive help content that empowers customers throughout their banking journey.

Collaborative workshop mapping out design solutions and priority areas

Following the workshop, I developed detailed customer journey maps and task flows to visualize and concretize our ideas. These journey maps brought life to concrete user scenarios, helping us identify pain points and opportunities throughout the mortgage application process. After aligning on the solutions to prototype and test, I created low-fidelity wireframes to validate our concepts before committing to detailed design work.

Customer journey mapping visualizing the end-to-end mortgage application experience

1

Information Architecture & User Flows

We simplified navigation from 5-level deep menus to a 2-level structure, reducing cognitive load and making key features accessible within 2 taps. This involved restructuring the entire banking app hierarchy to prioritize frequently used features while maintaining logical grouping of related functions.

Quick Access Panel

Most-used features available from home screen

Contextual Navigation

Dynamic menus based on user behavior patterns

Progressive Disclosure

Complex features revealed as needed

Search Integration

Universal search across all banking functions

2

Accessibility-First Design System

We established comprehensive accessibility guidelines meeting WCAG 2.1 AA standards, including color contrast ratios, touch target sizes, and screen reader compatibility. The design system was built from the ground up to ensure financial services were accessible to all customers regardless of ability.

Dynamic Type Scaling

Text scales up to 200% without breaking layout

High Contrast Mode

Multiple contrast themes for visual impairments

Voice Navigation

Complete voice control for all banking functions

Reduced Motion

Option to minimize animations for vestibular disorders

3

Security & Authentication Strategy

We implemented layered security with seamless biometric authentication, reducing login time from 45 to 8 seconds while maintaining bank-level security standards. This balanced approach maintained regulatory compliance while dramatically improving user experience.

Biometric Authentication

Face ID, Touch ID, and voice recognition options

Behavioral Analytics

AI-powered fraud detection and risk assessment

One-Tap Approvals

Simplified transaction confirmation processes

Encrypted Storage

Bank-level encryption for all sensitive data

Key Learnings

This project demonstrated how inclusive design principles can drive both social impact and business results in the financial sector:

Accessibility Drives Innovation

Designing for users with disabilities often creates better experiences for all users, as seen in our simplified navigation benefiting everyone.

Security Can Be Seamless

Biometric authentication and behavioral analytics can provide bank-level security without compromising user experience.

Regulatory Alignment

Proactively exceeding accessibility regulations positioned Barclays as an industry leader and reduced compliance risks.

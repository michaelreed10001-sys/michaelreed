Sciensus · Patient PSP app (greenfield)

Designing a modular, disease‑agnostic patient support programme app from scratch — combining human and digital care.

Company
Sciensus (acquired Vinehealth)
Role
Senior Product Designer
Timeline
8 months (v1.0)
Platform
iOS / Android + HCP portal

Mission

Craft intuitive, patient-first experiences that define the Sciensus PSP Platform across patient apps, HCP portals, and pharma insights tools. This greenfield project aimed to embed behavioural design and accessibility from day one — ultimately driving adoption, engagement, and trust in digital PSPs.

"Empowering pharma to launch globally scalable, disease‑agnostic patient support programmes that combine digital tools and nurse‑led care."

Core pillars (from the hybrid PSP proposition)

  • Human Support: Clinical nurse visits, patient support line, case management, AE monitoring.
  • Digital Support: Patient Companion App, nurse‑led management, insights & reporting.
  • HCP Portal: Secure view of patient progress, adherence data, and clinical notes.

Research & discovery

We conducted 22 interviews with patients (oncology, rare disease, MS), 8 nurses, and 5 pharma stakeholders. Shadowed 3 clinical home visits to understand real-world frictions.

Key insights

  • Anxiety around missed doses: 70% of patients worried about forgetting medication – need for discreet, persistent reminders.
  • Symptom tracking burden: Current paper diaries are often incomplete; digital should be predictive, not exhaustive.
  • Nurse visibility: Nurses felt blind between visits – they wanted alerts on deteriorating symptoms.
  • HCP portal must be separate: Clinicians need aggregated data, not another app.

Journey mapping workshop output – key moments of anxiety and opportunity.

Design process

1

Core user flows

We mapped three primary flows: 1) onboarding & consent, 2) daily medication confirmation + symptom check, 3) contacting the nurse. Each was optimised for low cognitive load and accessibility.

Onboarding – 5‑step consent & profile setup with clear data usage explanations.

Daily check‑in – confirm medication + quick symptom log.

Nurse contact – in‑app secure chat or schedule a call back.

HCP Portal – patient list, alerts, and clinical notes.

AE reporting – simple flow for patient to report side effects, with immediate nurse notification.

2

Information architecture

Patient app organised around "Today" (medication & tasks), "Track" (symptoms, vitals), "Care team" (nurse contact, visit schedule), and "Me" (settings, consents). HCP portal designed as a separate web app with dashboard, patient timeline, and reporting module.

Sketches & ideation

Wireframes & flows

5

Final screen flows

After usability testing (12 participants), we simplified the symptom log to 3 taps and added a "send to nurse" shortcut. The HCP portal flow reduced clicks to view patient history from 5 to 2.

Final solution

Patient Companion App (modular by design)

Built as a white‑label platform, the app adapts to each therapy area: oncology, neurology, or rare disease. Core modules include:

  • Smart medication reminders – adaptive to patient schedule, with escalation to nurse if 2 doses missed.
  • Symptom & wellbeing tracker – pattern recognition to help patients understand their health.
  • Nurse chat & video calls – integrated with the clinical case management system.
  • AE reporter – 1‑tap adverse event flag with automatic notification to pharmacovigilance.

HCP Portal & Insights

A web‑based portal gives nurses and clinicians a longitudinal view of each patient: adherence trends, symptom logs, and alert flags. Built‑in reporting tools allow pharma partners to analyse real‑world evidence (anonymised).

disease modules launched in first year

The greenfield PSP app was soft‑launched with two pharma partners (oncology and MS). Adherence improved significantly; nurses reported reduced inbound calls because they could proactively reach out. The design system enabled a second therapy module to be built in 6 weeks (instead of estimated 4 months).

Success measures (from the job description)

  • Improved adoption metrics: 78% of invited patients activated the app within 3 days.
  • Usability & accessibility: 96% system usability score (SUS) across 50 patients.
  • Design system reuse: v1.0 design system now used for two additional PSP builds.
  • Qualitative feedback: "Finally, an app that feels like it was made for me, not for a doctor." – Patient, MS trial.

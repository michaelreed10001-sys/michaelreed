UI/UX Design Theatre Experience Mobile App Case Study

ATG Theatre Booking App

A UX case study: Designing a mobile app to simplify theatre ticket booking for busy professionals who want to avoid queues and secure tickets easily.

My Role

Sole UX designer responsible for designing the theatre booking app from concept to delivery.

Responsibilities

Conducting interviews, wireframing, prototyping, usability studies, accessibility considerations, and design iteration.

Project Duration

July 2023 to August 2023 (8 weeks)

PROJECT OVERVIEW

Simplifying Theatre Ticket Booking

The Product

ATG Theatre Booking App is designed to simplify the process of booking theatre tickets through mobile phones. The app also offers the facility of ordering food and beverages along with ticket purchases. The app targets customers such as workers who don't have the time, prefer to avoid queues, or are worried about running out of tickets.

The Problem

Workers often face challenges in purchasing theatre tickets the conventional way. Long queues at ticket counters are time-consuming and inconvenient, especially for those with busy schedules. They feel reluctant to stand in long queues or worry that the tickets will run out if not purchased immediately. These frustrations make the ticket-buying experience less enjoyable and may reduce their interest in attending shows at theatres.

The Goal

The ATG Theatre Booking App provides a solution to this problem by offering a more efficient and practical theatre ticket booking application. With this app, users can book theatre tickets anytime and anywhere through their mobile phones.

UNDERSTANDING THE USER

Research and User Insights

User Interviews

Conducted interviews to understand the needs and pain points of workers when booking theatre tickets.

Persona Creation

Developed detailed user personas based on research findings to guide design decisions.

Journey Mapping

Mapped the complete user journey when booking tickets directly at the theatre to identify pain points.

Research Findings

Through interviews and research, I identified that the main user groups were workers who did not have time to buy tickets at the theatre. The research also revealed that time is not the only factor that restricts users from buying tickets at the theatre. Other user problems include not wanting to queue for too long and running out of tickets when booking directly at the venue.

Jane

Busy Branch Manager | Age 32

Goals

Wants to easily book theatre tickets without wasting time in queues. Needs flexibility to book from anywhere at any time.

Frustrations

Hates long queues at ticket counters. Worries about popular shows selling out. Finds it inconvenient to physically go to the theatre just to buy tickets.

Problem Statement

"Jane is a busy worker who works as a branch manager who needs theatre tickets easily because she doesn't have time, is lazy to queue, or is afraid of running out of tickets."

Discovery Phase

Users feel overwhelmed by multiple information sources and lack centralized show information.

Booking Process

Long queues and complex payment processes create significant frustration and anxiety.

Post-Booking Experience

USABILITY STUDY

Testing and Iteration

I conducted two rounds of usability studies. Findings from the first study helped guide the designs from wireframes to mockups. The second study used a high-fidelity prototype and revealed what aspects of the mockups needed refining.

Interactive Prototype

Given the mobile-first nature of the app, we decided to test mobile-first designs by creating an interactive prototype that simulated the complete ticket booking flow. This prototype allowed users to experience the app as if it were fully functional, enabling us to gather valuable insights on navigation, task completion, and overall user satisfaction.

Complete User Flow

The prototype included the entire booking journey from show discovery to payment confirmation.

Realistic Interactions

Interactive elements mimicked real app behavior, including button states, transitions, and feedback.

Usability Testing

The prototype was used in both rounds of usability studies to identify pain points and areas for improvement.

Open Prototype in New Tab

Round 1 Findings

Better navigation needed: Users needed better navigation to select shows/plays.
Location option requested: Users wanted a location option to view schedules in other cities.
Clearer promo code instructions: Users needed clearer instructions for where to enter the promo code.

Round 2 Findings

Animation improvement needed: The animation when sliding the show poster looked awkward and needed refinement.
Theatre company information: Users wanted to know the specific theatre company or venue (e.g., ATG, West End theatre name).
REFINING THE DESIGN

Implementing User Feedback

Based on usability study findings, I made several key improvements to the design:

1

Improved Show Selection

Early designs allowed users to select a show by tapping on the poster. After usability testing, I added a dedicated "Select" button to make the action more clear and intuitive.

2

Added Location Selector

Based on user feedback, I added a location option so users can easily view show schedules in different cities or regions.

3

Clearer Promo Code Field

To make it easier for users, I created a clearer, dedicated place to enter promo codes with a prominent "Apply" button.

4

Theatre Venue Information

The second usability study revealed that users have preferences for specific theatre venues, so I added information about the theatre company or venue to each listing.
